To do List
Summary:
Redesign the “Local Risk Assessment” campus summary cards for clarity, visual hierarchy, and quick scanning. Focus on a modern, readable, and less crowded layout that uses color to instantly communicate campus status.

Color Rules (Kulay):
- Safe = green
- Monitoring = orange
- Risk/Warning = red
Apply these colors to the status badge, card accent/border, summary banner, and any indicator icons.

Visual Representation:
- Each card shows:
  1. Campus name
  2. Status badge (colored by status)
  3. Short description sentence
  4. 3–5 key metrics (e.g., Rainfall, Humidity, Wind, Visibility, Temperature)
  5. Secondary metrics in a lighter, less prominent section
  6. Bottom summary banner (e.g., “No warning”, “Monitoring required”)
- Use strong spacing, large text for important info, minimal borders, and a modern, soft card style.
- The card’s accent/border and status badge/banner use the status color for instant recognition.
- Safe cards feel calm, monitoring cards are noticeable but not alarming, and risk cards are attention-grabbing.

Description:
The campus card layout is designed for instant readability and status recognition. The most important information (campus name, status, and a short description) is visually dominant. Key metrics are easy to scan, while technical details are de-emphasized. The card’s color accent and status badge/banner reflect the campus condition, making it easy for users to identify safe, monitoring, or risky campuses at a glance. The overall style is modern, soft, and consistent with BatStateU’s red risk-alert theme.
